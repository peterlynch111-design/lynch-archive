Due Diligence

Pre-transaction investigation supporting mergers, acquisitions, partnerships, and employment decisions through verification and risk identification.

2003–2026 · Lynch Investigations & Countermeasures

Service Overview

Due diligence services provided independent verification of representations and identification of undisclosed risks before significant business decisions. Whether hiring a key employee, entering a partnership, or acquiring a company, due diligence reduced the risk of costly surprises.

Investigations ranged from basic background checks to comprehensive corporate due diligence examining company history, principals, financial indicators, litigation history, and regulatory compliance.

Services Provided

Corporate Due Diligence

Comprehensive investigation of companies and their principals prior to mergers, acquisitions, partnerships, or significant contracts. Verification of representations, identification of undisclosed liabilities, and assessment of reputational risks.

Background Checks

Pre-employment screening and background investigation of individuals. Verification of qualifications, employment history, and identification of concerning information from public records and discreet enquiries.

Asset Searches

Identification of assets held by individuals and companies. Property holdings, company interests, vehicle registrations, and other assets discoverable through lawful search methods.

Reference Verification

Independent verification of references provided by job applicants, business partners, and contractors. Confirmation of relationships, roles, and performance with referees.

Credential Verification

Confirmation of claimed qualifications, professional registrations, memberships, and certifications. Identification of fraudulent or misrepresented credentials.

Litigation History

Searches of court records to identify civil litigation, bankruptcy, insolvency, and criminal matters involving subjects. Assessment of litigation risk and undisclosed legal issues.

Applications

Pre-Employment

Verification of candidates for senior positions, positions of trust, and roles with access to sensitive information or assets.

Mergers & Acquisitions

Investigation of acquisition targets and their principals beyond financial due diligence — reputation, litigation, and integrity issues.

Vendor Assessment

Evaluation of suppliers, contractors, and service providers before entering significant commercial relationships.

Investment

Investigation of investment opportunities and promoters. Verification of claims and identification of red flags before committing capital.

Information Sources

Due diligence investigations drew on multiple information sources:

  • Public records: ASIC company searches, property titles, court records, bankruptcy registers
  • Open source: Media searches, internet research, social media analysis
  • Database searches: Commercial databases, credit references, industry registers
  • Discreet enquiries: Industry contacts, references, professional networks
  • International: Partner network for overseas enquiries
