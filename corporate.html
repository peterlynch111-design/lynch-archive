Corporate Investigations

Comprehensive corporate security services addressing internal threats, fraud, theft, and workplace misconduct for Australian businesses.

2003–2026 · Lynch Investigations & Countermeasures

Service Overview

Corporate investigations addressed the internal security challenges facing Australian businesses — employee fraud, theft, policy violations, and workplace misconduct. These investigations supported HR decision-making, legal proceedings, and loss prevention efforts.

Peter Lynch's qualifications in fraud investigation (Post Graduate Certificate, Charles Sturt University) and forensic accounting provided specialist capability for complex financial investigations beyond standard private investigation work.

Services Provided

Corporate Intelligence

Background investigation of business partners, competitors, and potential associates. Due diligence support for mergers, acquisitions, and partnerships. Competitive intelligence within legal boundaries.

Corporate Fraud

Investigation of financial irregularities, embezzlement, expense fraud, procurement fraud, and accounting manipulation. Evidence gathering for civil recovery, criminal referral, and employment action.

Theft Investigation

Internal theft investigation covering inventory shrinkage, equipment theft, intellectual property misappropriation, and data theft. Identification of perpetrators and recovery of assets where possible.

Security Investigations

Breach investigations, access control failures, security policy violations, and incident response. Assessment of security vulnerabilities exposed by incidents and recommendations for improvement.

Loss Prevention

Systematic investigation of inventory losses, retail shrinkage, and unexplained shortages. Pattern analysis, vulnerability assessment, and preventive recommendations.

Undercover Operations

Covert investigation within workplaces to identify theft rings, policy violations, and misconduct. Carefully managed operations with appropriate legal and HR coordination.

Workplace Misconduct

Corporate investigations frequently addressed workplace misconduct matters requiring independent investigation:

  • Policy violations: Breach of employment conditions, conflicts of interest, secondary employment
  • Harassment & bullying: Workplace behaviour complaints requiring investigation
  • Time theft: Falsified timesheets, extended breaks, personal activities during work hours
  • Substance abuse: Workplace impairment affecting safety and performance
  • Misuse of resources: Vehicles, equipment, facilities, and IT systems

Investigation Process

Corporate investigations followed a structured methodology ensuring thoroughness, legal compliance, and evidentiary integrity:

  • Scoping: Definition of investigation objectives, constraints, and reporting requirements
  • Evidence gathering: Document collection, interviews, surveillance, and forensic analysis
  • Analysis: Evaluation of evidence, identification of patterns, and determination of facts
  • Reporting: Comprehensive written reports suitable for HR, legal, and executive audiences
  • Support: Assistance with disciplinary processes, legal proceedings, and recovery actions

Relevant Qualifications

Corporate investigation services were supported by specialist qualifications:

  • Post Graduate Certificate in Fraud Investigation — Charles Sturt University (2007)
  • Advanced Diploma of Government Fraud Control Management — Australian Forensic Services (2005)
  • Association of Certified Fraud Examiners (ACFE) membership
  • Certified Information Security Manager (CISM)
  • Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA)
